About Me


I am a focused, detail-oriented, and organized senior mechanical engineering (major) and computer science (minor) student at The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art (expected: May 2022) in New York City.

I am interested in pursuing a computational mechanical engineering PhD, specifically focusing on fluid mechanics with the use of numerical methods, simulations, and data science. I spent the summer of 2021 as an undergraduate R&D intern at Sandia National Laboratories in the Nonlinear Mechanics and Dynamics (NOMAD) Research Institute. There, I was one of eighteen students in the program and one of only six undergraduate students. I worked on FEA simulations (using Sierra/SM on Sandiaâ€™s HPC servers) of a material puncture scenario and then fit the data in Python using nonlinear least-squares regression with a previously developed empirical puncture energy equation. As I did during my internship and also do in many of my mechanical engineering classes, I utilize Python and other programming languages to further develop the various projects I have worked on, writing programs to aid in optimization and simulation.

Some of my favorite undergraduate classes included: fluid mechanics, vibrations, numerical methods, mechanics of materials, stress & applied elasticity, and heat transfer. I have also taken a number of graduate-level electives, notably: computer-aided engineering (CAE), computational fluid dynamics (CFD), finite element methods (FEM), advanced fluid mechanics, and machine learning. As part of a rigorous Ph.D. program, I hope to continue working and researching in the area of FEA/CFD, perfectly combining my mechanical engineering and computer science interests.
